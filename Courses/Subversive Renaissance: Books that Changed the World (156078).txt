Subversive Renaissance: Books that Changed the World (156078)
Katharina Piechocki
2018 Spring (4 Credits) Schedule: M 0200 PM - 0359 PM Instructor Permissions: None Enrollment Cap: n/a
This course explores major books from the Renaissance and Baroque period (14th-17th centuries). It focuses on texts that were revolutionary when first published or/and that (still) have a significant impact on us now. We will discover fourteenth-century Persian poets and sonnets from sixteenth-century Poland and seventeenth-century Mexico; proto-feminist texts in France, the rise of theater in Italy, subversive short stories in Spain, utopian visions in Francis Bacon, and the limits of the human in Erasmus and Montaigne. Authors include Christine de Pizan, Boccaccio, Jahan Malek Khatun, Calderon, Petrarch, Sor Juana, Kochanowski, Machiavelli, Veronica Franco, Beccadelli, Madeleine de l'Aubespine, and Cervantes.
