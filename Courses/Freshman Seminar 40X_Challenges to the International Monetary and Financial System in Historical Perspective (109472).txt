Challenges to the International Monetary and Financial System in Historical Perspective (109472)
Kenneth Rogoff
2017 Fall (4 Credits) Schedule: M 0400 PM - 0559 PM Instructor Permissions: Instructor Enrollment Cap: 12
This seminar explores contemporary debates on the future of the international monetary and financial system drawing on both historical and recent experiences. Topics will include understanding the underpinning and aftermath of the Great Depression of the 1930s and the financial crisis of 2008, including the slow growth and impotency of monetary policy after both episodes.
Recommended Prep: The course does not require any background in economics.
