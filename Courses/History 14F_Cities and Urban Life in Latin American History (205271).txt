This undergraduate seminar examines the city in Latin American history from the colonial period to the present. Topics include the organization of pre-Columbian cities, the role of cities in the establishment and subversion of colonial authority, urban slavery, efforts to impose discipline on the city and the response of urban dwellers, planned and unplanned urban spaces, criminality, and the formation of urban identities. This seminar also analyzes the role of cities in the construction of rights and explores the city as a site of creativity. A wide range of educational materials will be covered including maps, photographs, paintings, and films.
