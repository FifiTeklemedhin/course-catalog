Foundations in Acting: Viewpoints (112880)
Thomas Derrah
2018 Spring (4 Credits) Schedule: MW 1130 AM - 1259 PM Instructor Permissions: Instructor Enrollment Cap: 20
An exploration of the basic techniques of acting, beginning with exercises that flex the imagination and heighten observation; the course will then move towards work on rhythm, an actor's instincts, focus, concentration, and character with an ongoing emphasis in improvisation. The texts of Anton Chekhov will be used as a point of reference for the work. The latter part of the course will concentrate on selected scene study from Chekhov's major plays. Especially suitable for freshmen and sophomores.
Course Notes: Enrollment determined by audition. Students will audition for the course with a short improvisation described by the professor at the first class meeting.
