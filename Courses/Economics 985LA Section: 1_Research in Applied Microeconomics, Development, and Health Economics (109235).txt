Research in Applied Microeconomics, Development, and Health Economics (109235)
Kiran Gajwani
2017 Fall (4 Credits) Schedule: TBD Instructor Permissions: Instructor Enrollment Cap: n/a
Workshop for seniors writing theses. Especially for topics in development or health economics, but open to all fields. Emphasis on choice of research topics, methodology, and data sources. Part one of a two-part series; students must complete both terms of this course (parts A and B) within the same academic year in order to receive credit.
