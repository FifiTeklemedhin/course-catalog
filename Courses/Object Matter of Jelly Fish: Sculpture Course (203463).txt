 Arts and Humanities
         Central issues of this studio course will be sculptural representation beyond the human scale and the special relationship between matter and meaning in sculptural production. The course investigates in the notion of „the other" in sculptural production and the possibility of sculpture to involve in different contexts of our time and reality, ranging from the moment when an object is liberated from its original circumstances of production to its continuing life and how it might reflect on new situations.
Course Notes: Pre-requisite: At least one VES studio course or permission of instructor. Interested students must attend first meeting of class during shopping week
to speak with teaching staff about course enrollment procedure.
