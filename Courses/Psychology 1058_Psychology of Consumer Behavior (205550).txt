         Psychological theory and research findings have wide and varied applications to almost every other field; the world of business marketing is no exception. Understanding the thoughts, attitudes, and behaviors of consumers is imperative for the success of businesses, governments, and, in fact, anyone else looking to influence others. The purpose of this course is to provide working knowledge of psychological concepts related to consumer behavior including but not limited to memory, attitudes, preferences, judgments, and decision making. In addition, the course aims to use case studies and research projects to develop the ability to apply and evaluate influential strategies that make use of these psychological concepts.
