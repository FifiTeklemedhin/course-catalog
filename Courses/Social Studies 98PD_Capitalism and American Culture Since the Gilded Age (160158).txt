Capitalism and American Culture Since the Gilded Age (160158)
Ryan Acton
2018 Spring (4 Credits) Schedule: M 0400 PM - 0559 PM Instructor Permissions: Instructor Enrollment Cap: 10
This course asks how capitalism and American culture have shaped each other since the rise of the modern economy after the Civil War. To what extent and in what ways has culture shaped economic life? To what extent and in what ways has economic life shaped the desires, aspirations, and values of Americans? Topics may include: evangelical businessmen, investment banking, race and suburban development, Walmart, cosmetic products, women in the corporate workplace, masculinity and the self-made man, the cult of celebrity, the "American Dream," and the astrology column of the LA Times. This is a junior tutorial.
