It Takes a Village: Child Development and Social Policy (204545)
Sarah Hope Lincoln
2018 Spring (4 Credits) Schedule: TBD Instructor Permissions: Instructor Enrollment Cap: n/a
Will investing in the education of its children change a community's economic climate? Should lead screening be required for all children to prevent lead poisoning such as occurred in Flint, Michigan? What is the effect of poverty on a child's cognitive functioning? Which policies exploit children and which policies protect them? This course examines the structure and implementation of social policy to solve problems related to children, using the lens of developmental psychology.
Recommended Prep: The Psychology Department requires completion of Science of Living Systems 20 (or equivalent) before enrolling in this course.
