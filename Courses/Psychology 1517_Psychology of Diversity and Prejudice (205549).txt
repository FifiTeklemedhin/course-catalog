         Diversity, broadly defined, is a complex and contentious subject, particularly in psychological research. This course aims to examine various identities (including race, culture, gender, class, sexual orientation, religion, and disability) and the insights that psychological research can provide about them, and the intersections between them. As a group, we will come to a better understanding of psychological theory related to diversity (such as stereotyping and prejudice), scrutinize the role of diversity in the content and methods of psychological research, and reflect on the impact of diversity through personal experiences in order to facilitate an interest in examining these social
