Globalization and Civil Society (108238)
Susan Pharr
2018 Spring (4 Credits) Schedule: R 0300 PM - 0459 PM Instructor Permissions: Instructor Enrollment Cap: 16
This seminar examines the rise of civil society worldwide and the role civil society organizations play in shaping social, economic, and political realities. It explores the origins of the concept; the relation between civil society and democracy; the complex roles of civil society in developed democracies, in democratic transitions, under conditions of repression, and in transborder advocacy and development. Cases include: Europe, U.S., Japan, Korea, Taiwan, and China.
