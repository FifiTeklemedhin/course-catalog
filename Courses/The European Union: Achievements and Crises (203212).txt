The European Union: Achievements and Crises (203212)
Charles Maier Hans-Helmut Kotz
2017 Fall (4 Credits) Schedule: Instructor Permissions: Instructor Enrollment Cap:
W 0200 PM - 0359 PM 20
A conference course that examines the history and economics of the European Union from the late 1940s through its institutional changes, accomplishments and current crises: Coal and Steel Community, Common Market, agricultural policies, constitutional treaties, monetary union, the common frontier, Euro and migration issues. Students will prepare a final research paper.
Course Notes: Crossed-listed in Economics. This course counts towards the secondary field in European History, Politics, and Societies (EHPS).
