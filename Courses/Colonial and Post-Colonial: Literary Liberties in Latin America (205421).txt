           Hierarchies established by the Conquest continue to color Latin American culture, often along racial lines. Independence often meant replacing Iberian bureaucrats with rich white Creoles. And abolition never managed to abolish the habits of racism practiced so long they can seem normal, unremarkable. But literary liberties unsettle
