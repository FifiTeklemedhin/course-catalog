This interdisciplinary course will examine Asian American literature and film alongside political and historical readings about how Asian Americans have taken action as artists, activists, workers, politicians, criminals, etc. We will consider issues including: gender and sexuality, immigration history/American Dream, ethnic and class conflicts within Asian America, the problem of "authenticity," the effects of America's wars in Asia, the particular stereotypes associated with Asian Americans, and Asian American positions in relation to other American minorities. Text modes and genres will range widely and may include: comedy, anger, sentiment, nostalgia, and commitment in novels, social theory, poetry, legal studies, journalism, personal narrative, film.
