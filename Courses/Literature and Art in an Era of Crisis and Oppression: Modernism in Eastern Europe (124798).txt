Literature and Art in an Era of Crisis and Oppression: Modernism in Eastern Europe (124798)
George Grabowicz
2017 Fall (4 Credits) Schedule: M 0200 PM - 0359 PM Instructor Permissions: None Enrollment Cap: n/a
The course will examine seminal literary works (with forays into film and art) from Eastern Europe in the first half of the 20th century with special attention to their response to convention, censorship and totalitarian strictures as well as "high modernist" experimentation and a "low modernist" focus on popular genres and a new poetics of trash. Focus on Kafka, Zamiatin, Bulgakov, Capek, Nabokov, Platonov, Witkacy, Schulz, Gombrowicz, Kulish, Xvyl'ovyi, Vertov, Dovzhenko and others.
